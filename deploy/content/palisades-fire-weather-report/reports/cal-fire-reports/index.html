Palisades Fire Weather Analysis – Potential Containment Window (10:00 a.m.–4:00 p.m., January 7 2025)

Cal Fire records show the fire held at 200 acres while sustained winds peaked at just 35.7 mph—conditions historically considered manageable for fire‑ground operations.

Executive Summary

Between 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. on January 7 2025, the Palisades Fire expanded modestly—from 10 acres to 200 acres—while wind speeds remained moderate. The highest sustained wind recorded nearby was 35.7 mph, with gusts up to 44 mph, well below thresholds that typically render wildfire suppression impossible. Assertions that the blaze was instantly uncontrollable due to extreme winds in this window are not supported by the data.

Wind Conditions During the Critical Six‑Hour Window

Throughout the 10 a.m.–4 p.m. period, Malibu’s peak sustained wind was 35.7 mph, with gusts peaking at 44 mph. Both values fall within historically typical Santa Ana events and remain far below the 50–60 mph range often associated with rapidly uncontrollable fire behavior.

Implications for Fire Management

The overlap of manageable wind speeds and limited acreage growth suggests that containment efforts during this period may have benefitted from different resource deployments or strategies. This analysis underscores the importance of accurate real‑time data interpretation when making suppression decisions.

Key Takeaways

  1. The Palisades Fire remained comparatively small (≤ 200 acres) until after 2:30 p.m.
  2. Wind conditions stayed within historical norms, undermining claims of unprecedented, uncontrollable weather.
  3. The 10 a.m.–4 p.m. window represents a critical opportunity for containment that warrants closer review by incident commanders and policymakers.
